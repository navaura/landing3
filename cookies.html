1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. The website cannot function properly without these cookies.

2.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies track your online activity to help advertisers deliver more relevant advertising or to limit how many times you see an ad. These cookies can share that information with other organizations or advertisers.

4. How to Manage Cookies

4.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

4.2 Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

4.3 Opt-Out Tools

You can also opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on

5. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable or refuse cookies, please note that:

  • Some parts of the website may become inaccessible or not function properly
  • Your user experience may be degraded
  • Personalized features may not work
  • You may see less relevant content

Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the website to function.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, our website does not respond to DNT signals, as there is no industry standard for how websites should respond to such signals.

7. Mobile Devices

On mobile devices, you can manage cookie preferences through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies
  • Android: Settings → Site Settings → Cookies
